Selected Strategic Work

Mixed 1981 — Auckland CBD Café

Sample Engagement
Mixed 1981 is a Korean-inspired café in Auckland CBD with a genuinely rare "blue ocean" advantage: extended late-night hours and high-quality seating — something almost no other café in the area offers. I was brought in to figure out why that advantage wasn't translating into foot traffic or brand recognition.
This engagement is currently paused before execution — shown here as a full sample of the diagnostic, strategy, and action plan I deliver, not as a completed results case study.
01The Problem: An "Identity Gap"

Mixed 1981 had the ingredients for a standout brand, but the public perception didn't match the opportunity. The brand was sitting between four different identities at once — study café, dessert café, Korean-inspired café, and late-night café — which created confusion and weak recall instead of a clear reason to choose it.

Current Perception
The Opportunity
A "hidden gem" people stumble upon, rather than seek out
A destination staple for Auckland's creative & student community
Caught between study café, dessert café, Korean café and late-night café — confusing, weak recall
One clear line: the "Seoul-Inspired Urban Retreat" for deep work
Functional late-night hours, but no defined night-time experience, behavioural triggers, or content reinforcing "night culture"
An intentional night-time experience people actively plan around — the "Sunset Switch"
02The Strategy: Three Pillars
Pillar I

Defining the "Mixed 1981 Tribe"

To move beyond a generic café, the brand needed to define who it's for — not just what it sells.

  • Target persona: the "Ambitious Creative" and the "Deep-Work Student."
  • Brand promise: "Auckland's Second Wind" — a space for people who find their flow after the rest of the city closes.
  • Social proof shift: from photos of coffee to photos of people in their element — laptops, notebooks, and signature drinks in a focused environment — making the night-time usage visible and aspirational.
Pillar II

The "Night-Time Destination"

Being open late is a function. Making it a destination is the marketing solution — shifting from selling "late hours" to selling "the night-time vibe."

A. Marketing the "Sunset Switch"
The After-Hours Collective
  • A content series showcasing high-aesthetic "study-with-me" and working-creative snapshots of the evening atmosphere
  • Captions that define the guest — "the lights are low, the focus is high" — turning a visit into an identity statement, not just a coffee run
B. Building "Why Go Tonight?"
Night-only features & environment shift
  • "Midnight Fuel" combos and caffeine-free "Night-Cap" Korean herbal teas/ades, branded specifically for evening visitors
  • At 6pm, the café shifts from Day Mode to Focus Mode: dimmed overheads, warm desk lamps, and a curated "Seoul Night" lo-fi/jazz playlist
Pillar III

Digital Precision & Premium Branding

Because Mixed 1981 is a "hidden gem," the first impression happens on Maps and Instagram before anyone walks in — consistency there is what protects a premium price point.

A. Google Maps cleanup
Eliminate confusion
  • Remove outdated photos from the café's former identity that dilute the premium Seoul aesthetic
  • Upload a high-density photo set so the first five images a searcher sees are current, high-quality, and on-brand
B. Instagram — the "Rule of Two"
Consistent, curated feed
  • Lock in two fonts for all stories and posts, for instant brand recognition
  • Follow a consistent "Product → Person → Space" posting rhythm so the grid reads as intentional, not random

Brand Promise

"Auckland's Second Wind."

03Summary of Recommended Actions
→

Brand positioning: reposition the marketing to focus on the user — creatives and students — rather than just the menu.

→

Experience marketing: launch the "Sunset Switch" campaign so the evening hours read as an intentional vibe, not just a functional convenience.

→

Digital audit: cleanse Google Maps of outdated branding and unify Instagram typography immediately.
